1. # brian


## who he is


brian is a vietnamese entrepreneur and one of the earliest people connected to my builder phase. he is the co-founder of duodode and hyped indie, and someone i’ve spent a lot of time with through work, conversations, and building things together.


he is not the type of person who shows everything immediately. at first, he can feel reserved and a bit hard to read. but over time, it becomes clear that he thinks deeply about the things he cares about, especially around work and direction. his strength shows more through consistency and how he approaches problems than through how he presents himself.


in my archive, brian belongs to the group of people who shaped me through proximity. he was there during a phase where i was still figuring things out, and a lot of what i learned came from being in the process with him.


---


## how we met


i met brian at work, around the time i had just graduated and moved from trade marketing into digital marketing.


he onboarded around the same time as me. i remember that period clearly because i had already started taking my life more seriously. i had a direction in mind, and i was trying to align my work, learning, and habits with it.


at the same time, my actual skills were still limited. i had ideas, i had read a lot, but i was still early in turning those ideas into something real.


brian entered my life at that stage.


---


## first impression


my first impression of brian was that he seemed difficult to approach.


he didn’t talk much about himself, and conversations could feel a bit awkward when the topic didn’t interest him. there was a kind of distance in how he communicated.


then there were moments where the topic shifted into something he cared about, and everything changed. he became very articulate, clear, and thoughtful. that contrast was unexpected and stayed with me.


we ended up becoming friends quite naturally because we shared similar interests.


---


## what we do together


most of our interaction revolves around work.


we spend a lot of time talking through ideas, testing directions, and figuring out what makes sense to build next. sometimes it’s structured, sometimes it’s just long conversations that slowly turn into action.


there are also moments where we just sit, eat, drink, and talk about life. those conversations don’t always lead to something immediate, but they still shape how we think over time.


working together also means constantly adjusting. when i move too fast, he tends to slow things down and refine them. that dynamic became part of how we operate.


---


## what we’ve done together


- **new year resolutions** — we once sat together, ate, drank, and talked about the kind of year we wanted to have.
- **overnight meetings** — some of our conversations stretched late into the night, usually around work and direction.
- **off street coffee sessions** — brian introduced me to off street, a coffee place where we spent time working together.
- **gym sessions** — we trained together, which became part of the routine.
- **lunches during work days** — simple moments that became part of the friendship.
- **overnight hackathons** — we worked through intense sessions together trying to build things.
- **catan and board games** — brian taught me how to play catan, and we joined board game club sessions together.
- **clash league of legends** — we won a clash tournament with jack and jake.


---


## what stands out


what stands out most about brian is how he approaches work.


he is consistent, and he takes things seriously. when he commits to something, he stays with it. there is also a level of willingness to take calculated risks, which is something i relate to.


he is also quite structured in how he thinks. when i bring in new ideas or directions, he tends to slow things down and iterate. he organizes, refines, and makes things clearer.


over time, that had an effect on me. i became more aware of how i structure my own thinking.


from what i’ve observed, he is also very family-oriented, which reflects a sense of responsibility that goes beyond just work.


---


## what he means to me


brian is one of the most impactful people in my development.


a lot of that impact came through working together. conversations, projects, failed attempts, and shared experiences. things like hyped indie, duodode, and the process of trying to build something real.


he was there during a phase where i moved from having ideas into actually trying to execute them. that phase was messy, and having someone going through it at the same time mattered.


when i think about brian, i think about someone who was present in the early stage of my builder path. someone who influenced how i think about work, consistency, and what it actually takes to build something.


that’s why he belongs here.

18. # friedrich nietzsche


## who he is


friedrich nietzsche is a philosopher.


for me, he is the one i keep coming back to.


---


## how i found him


i was already interested in philosophy during university.


i had read different authors before. marcus aurelius, camus, kafka, dostoevsky, sylvia plath. each of them had something, but none of them felt fully “mine”.


then i came across nietzsche.


i don’t remember it as a clean starting point. it was more fragmented. reading about him first, then watching videos, especially one from :contentReference[oaicite:0]{index=0} titled *“nietzsche: the man who vibe checked god.”*


that video stayed.


from there, i started going deeper. quotes first, then books. *twilight of the idols*, *the gay science*, *beyond good and evil*.


it wasn’t easy. i struggled a lot reading him at the beginning. things didn’t always make sense immediately.


but something kept pulling me back.


---


## why he stayed


nietzsche was the first philosopher that felt personal.


reading him didn’t feel like learning ideas. it felt like being challenged.


there was a kind of honesty and intensity in his writing that didn’t let me stay comfortable. it forced me to think more seriously about myself, about what i was doing, and about how i was living.


with other authors, i understood.


with nietzsche, i reacted.


---


## what i took from him


one of the ideas that stayed with me is self-overcoming.


the idea that you are not fixed. that you are something to be worked on, shaped, and pushed further.


another is not living cheaply.


not settling for easy thinking, easy decisions, or an easy version of life. taking responsibility for what you could become.


and the simplest one that stayed the longest:


“he who has a why can bear almost any how.”


that line changed how i approach difficulty.


instead of avoiding pressure, i started asking if the reason behind what i’m doing is strong enough.


---


## where i disagree


there are parts of nietzsche that feel too extreme.


some ideas feel difficult to apply directly in real life, especially when it comes to relationships and how people actually live together.


i don’t take everything from him as something to follow exactly.


i take what helps me think more clearly and leave the rest.


---


## how he shows up in my life


nietzsche shows up in the way i think.


when i feel weak, when things get difficult, or when i start questioning direction, i often think about how he would look at the situation.


not as a quote, but as a standard.


he represents a way of thinking that refuses to stay passive.


he reminds me to take responsibility, to push further, and to not stay comfortable for too long.


it’s less about philosophy as knowledge, and more about philosophy as pressure.


something that keeps asking:


are you actually becoming who you could be?
