# having a direction


## what sits underneath everything


for me, it comes down to having a clear direction.


not something vague, not just “i want to be successful,” but a goal that i can lock into. something i can return to even when things get slow or unclear.


i don’t really like the word “ambition.” it feels overused, and people usually attach too many surface-level meanings to it. what matters more to me is having a direction that feels real enough to commit to.


if i strip it down, what sits underneath that direction is simple.


i want freedom.


not in a loud or performative way, but in a very specific sense. the ability to walk into a room and walk out of it if i want. to not feel tied down. to not feel like i’m forced into something i didn’t choose.


that’s the core.


---


## the life i can already see


the direction in my head is not fully detailed, but it’s clear enough.


i don’t want to be tied to one place. i want to live in different cities, experience different environments, and learn from them. dubai, new york, san francisco, tokyo. places where things move, where people build, where there’s energy.


my days are not complicated in that vision.


i work on my business. i read. i train. i take care of how i present myself. i spend time with people i actually respect. i keep learning.


there’s structure, but also space.


i don’t know exactly how much control i’ll have. and i’m not naive about it. if what i build actually matters and creates impact, i know i might have to give up some freedom for it.


and i’m okay with that.


---


## what makes this different


what i want is not just about having more.


i don’t need a lot just to buy things. that part doesn’t really motivate me.


what matters more is access.


access to different layers of life. different environments. different kinds of people. the ability to move beyond where i started. to go further, not just financially, but in terms of experience, understanding, and exposure.


in a simple way, it’s about taste.


you can have taste, but it only fully develops when you actually have access. when you can be in the right places, see the right things, be around the right people.


that’s part of what i’m moving toward.


---


## the price


i don’t think this comes without trade-offs.


time, comfort, solitude, being misunderstood, being doubted, pressure. all of that is part of it.


relationships can be affected. the path is not always easy to explain to other people. sometimes it doesn’t even look like it’s working from the outside.


but i’ve already accepted that.


the alternative is worse.


---


## what i’m actually afraid of


i’m not afraid of failing.


i’m more afraid of living a normal life that i didn’t choose.


that’s the real fear.


---


## staying on the path


the hardest part is not starting.


it’s continuing when results don’t show up immediately.


when you’re working on something long-term, there are long periods where nothing obvious happens. no clear reward, no external validation.


that’s where it’s easy to drift.


i’ve felt that. the temptation to switch directions, to chase something that looks faster or easier.


but the only thing that really matters is continuing to move, even when it’s slow.


---


## what makes it worth it


i don’t think you can sustain this without enjoying the process in some way.


not everything is fun. a lot of it is repetitive, tiring, sometimes frustrating.


but there are moments that make it worth it.


when i see that i’ve improved. when i unlock a new capability. when something i built actually works. when i hit a goal that used to feel out of reach.


even something simple like running.


there was a time i couldn’t run 5km continuously. now i’ve completed a half marathon. that shift doesn’t happen overnight. it comes from showing up again and again.


that’s what “enjoying the process” really means to me.


seeing that i’m not the same as before.


---


## where this leads


if i look at it honestly, it’s not about a single end point.


it’s about moving in a direction that feels right, and staying with it long enough for it to become something real.


learning, building, adjusting, continuing.


and becoming the kind of person who can actually hold the life i’m aiming for.


that matters more than any label.
