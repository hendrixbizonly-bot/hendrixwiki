# Designer vs Builder

Hendrix refuses to be boxed in as only a designer. He is a builder who uses design as one lever among several. This article is about that distinction and why it matters to him.

## The refusal

Calling him a designer underserves how he thinks. Design is one of his tools. So is [[Tech]]. So is [[Business Strategy]]. So is [[AI Automation]]. Limiting his identity to "designer" would force him to optimize for the wrong scoreboard: design awards, design community approval, craft for craft's sake.

He is not optimizing for that. He is optimizing for [[Build and Ship|shipping things that work]] and [[Money|building real wealth]].

## What a builder does

- Sees the whole system, not one layer
- Pulls whichever lever creates the biggest outcome
- Learns enough of every discipline to ship
- Does not wait for permission from specialists
- Treats tools, including design, as leverage (see [[Design As Leverage]])

## Why he cares about this

Most people who are good at design get trapped inside design. They become senior designers, design leads, design directors. Each step is a narrower box. Hendrix does not want that career shape. He wants ownership, scale, and [[Long-term Upside|long-term upside]], which come from operating across layers.

This is also why [[Duodode]] is positioned as a digital agency, not a design studio. The agency sells outcomes, which require more than design to produce.

## The tension

There is a tension here. Going wide risks being shallow. Hendrix handles it by going deep where it counts (design, tech, building) and refusing to pretend depth where it does not exist. He would rather be a builder with real range than a designer with decorative range.

## How it connects

This frame is the personal version of [[Design As Leverage]]. It connects to [[Build and Ship]], [[Execution Over Talk]], and [[ROI-Driven Thinking]]. It is part of why [[Duodode]] exists in the shape it does.
