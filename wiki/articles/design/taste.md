# Taste

Taste is Hendrix's word for the judgment layer that sits on top of craft. It is what separates a designer who knows the rules from a designer who knows when to break them and when not to.

## His specific take

Taste is not a vibe. It is a trained ability to tell real from fake, sharp from dull, useful from decorative. It is built through exposure and reps, not by reading about it.

He treats taste as a form of [[Competence]]. A person with taste can look at a page and see the three things that need to change before the client does. A person without taste copies what looks safe and calls it a style.

## Taste without substance

Hendrix is allergic to taste that is not tied to value. Pretty work that does not [[Business Value|sell, convert, clarify, or compound]] is not tasteful to him. It is expensive decoration. See [[Substance over Noise]].

Taste has to earn its place the same way design does. If it does not create an outcome, it is an indulgence.

## How he sharpens it

- Long-form reading, [[Essays]], [[Substack]], [[The New York Times]]
- Studying systems he respects, not just screenshots
- Watching [[YouTube]] creators with real depth like [[The Futur]] and [[Code Grid]]
- Doing the work. Shipping. Getting feedback. Adjusting.

## Why it matters to him

Taste is a compounding asset. Good taste makes every decision slightly faster and slightly better. Across a year of work that compounds into a visible gap between his output and the median. That gap is what he sells through [[Duodode]].

## How it connects

Taste informs [[Intentional Design]]. It is enforced by [[Visual Rigor]]. It is what keeps [[Timeless Design]] from becoming boring. It is the reason a [[No-BS]] approach can still produce beautiful work.
