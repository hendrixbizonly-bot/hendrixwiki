# Self-Learning

[[Self-Learning]] is one of the habits I trust most because it changes the shape of a life without asking permission first. It is how interests become skills, and how curiosity turns into actual range.

The idea became sharper for me through people like [[Shaurya]]. Watching someone much younger build real technical depth on his own made the principle harder to romanticize and easier to respect. Self-learning only matters when it produces understanding, taste, or real output.

For me, it has never lived in one lane. It moves through [[English]], business, design, technology, and whatever else starts pulling my attention. That is why I connect it not only to books or videos, but also to work itself. [[Digital Marketing]] taught me one version of it. Building taught me another.

What I value most is the independence behind it. Self-learning is a quiet refusal to wait for a classroom, a manager, or a perfect plan before getting started.
