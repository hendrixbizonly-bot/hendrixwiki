# Agency vs Product

Agency vs product is the strategic tension sitting underneath [[Duodode]]. Both models are legitimate. Both compound in different ways. For [[Hendrix]], the question is not which is objectively better. The question is which one fits where he is right now and how the two relate over time.

## The two models

- **Agency** - trade execution for money. Fast revenue, direct market feedback, capped by delivery capacity unless systematized.
- **Product** - trade time and capital upfront for an asset. Slow start, but uncapped [[Scale]] and strong [[Ownership]] if it works.

## Why agency first

[[Duodode]] is an agency because it fits his current position: strong craft, real [[Taste]], working technical range, and no interest in burning years without feedback. An agency earns while he builds, exposes him to real businesses, and sharpens [[Business Strategy]].

See [[Digital Agency]].

## Why product eventually

The long arc is larger than service revenue. Hendrix wants [[Ownership]], [[Scale]], and real [[Leverage]]. Products are often where those compound fastest. See [[Millionaire Goal]] and [[Long-term Goals]].

## How the two can connect

- Agency cash flow funds product bets
- Agency client work exposes product-shaped problems
- [[Productized Services]] act as a halfway step
- [[AI Automation]] and [[Systems]] built for clients can be spun into internal products

The agency is not in opposition to the product path. It is the ramp.

## The trap

Running an agency while pretending to build a product usually produces neither. The honest version is sequencing: build the agency well, earn the right to build the product, then actually do it.
