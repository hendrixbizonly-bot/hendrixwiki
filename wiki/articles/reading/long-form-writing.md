# Long-form Writing

Long-form writing is [[Hendrix]]'s preferred intellectual diet. He reads it to sharpen perspective, judgment, and language. Shallow content does not do that. Long-form does.

## Why he leans toward it

- Ideas need room to develop
- Good thinking is rarely compressed into a headline
- Long-form exposes you to a writer's actual point of view
- It is harder to fake depth at length, which makes the good stuff easier to recognize

## Where it lives for him

- [[Books]]
- [[Essays]] on [[Substack]], [[Medium]], and [[The New York Times]]
- [[Science Fiction]] and serious non-fiction
- [[Philosophy]], especially [[Nietzsche]]
- [[Video Essays]] as the video-native version of the same instinct

## What he avoids

He avoids shallow takes dressed up as insight. He avoids [[Fluff]] and fake-deep tone. If a long piece is just a short piece with padding, he drops it quickly.

## How it feeds his work

Long-form reading shows up in how he writes, how he thinks about [[Duodode]], and how he frames decisions. It supports [[English Goals|better articulation]] and keeps his thinking grounded in real substance.
