# Joma Tech

Joma Tech is a YouTube channel that sits at the intersection of tech career content and humor. For me, it is a lighter but still useful window into the software engineering world.

## What it is

Videos on tech careers, interviews, engineering culture, and life as a software engineer, often told with comedy and satire.

## Why I watch

I am a builder working in [[Tech]] and [[Entrepreneurship]]. Joma Tech provides:

- Perspective on the SWE world
- A humor layer on top of tech reality
- Career and industry context
- Light reinforcement of what [[Silicon Valley]] satirizes

## What it gives me

- Cultural context for the tech industry
- A sense of what the career paths actually look like
- A reminder that humor and tech mix well
- Occasional practical insight

## How it fits

Joma Tech is not one of my deep learning channels like [[Fireship]] or [[Kurzgesagt]]. It is more of a supporting input: entertainment that happens to run through my working world. That is useful too. Not everything needs to be dense.

## Connection to my system

Joma Tech is a reminder that tech culture has shape, texture, and humor worth understanding. For a founder operating inside it from outside the usual geographies, that texture is part of [[Leverage]]. See [[YouTube as Learning Platform]].
