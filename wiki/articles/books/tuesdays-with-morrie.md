# Tuesdays with Morrie — Mitch Albom

A memoir about conversations on life, death, and meaning between a former student and his dying professor.

## Essay

This is one of those books that clears the room a little. The things that look urgent start to look less important next to illness, time, and the simple question of how a person should live while he still can.

I keep it close to [[Mortality]] and [[What Lasts]] because that is the pressure it brings. It does not make me think about success first. It makes me think about warmth, attention, relationship, and whether a life feels inhabited in the right way. I respect books that can do that without sounding fake.
