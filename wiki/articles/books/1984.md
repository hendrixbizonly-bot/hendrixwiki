# 1984 — George Orwell

A dystopian novel about surveillance, control, and the manipulation of truth.

## Essay

The deepest unease in 1984 comes from how thoroughly truth can be managed. Surveillance is frightening, brutality is frightening, but the more chilling move is smaller than both: narrowing language until people gradually lose the ability to think clearly about what is being done to them.

That is why the book connects so strongly to [[Language as Thought]] for me. If language degrades, thought degrades with it. 1984 made that feel less like an abstract theory and more like a civilizational warning. It is one of those books that keeps making the value of clear thinking feel serious.
