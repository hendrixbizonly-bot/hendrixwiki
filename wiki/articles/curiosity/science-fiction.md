# Science Fiction

Science fiction is one of [[Hendrix]]'s favorite ways to stretch imagination and perspective. He reads it not for escape but for ideas. Good sci-fi collides science, philosophy, and human nature in one move, which is exactly the mix he is drawn to.

## Why it fits him

Hendrix prefers [[Long-form Writing|long-form]] ideas over shallow content. Sci-fi is a format built for that. It gives him room to sit inside a concept, watch it play out, and pull the useful pieces back into how he thinks and builds.

## What he looks for

- Books and stories that expand imagination
- Ideas that combine [[Science and Philosophy]]
- Worlds that create real [[Scale and Depth|scale]]
- Thought experiments about [[Future of Humanity|where humanity is going]]
- Writing that is sharp and takes itself seriously

## How it connects to his thinking

Sci-fi plays to the same instinct as [[Space]], [[Astronomy]], and [[Philosophy]]. It is a place where [[Existential Reflection]] meets technology, identity, and consequence. Many of the ideas he encounters through sci-fi sit near the ones he picks up from [[Nietzsche]] and long video essays.

## How it shows up in his life

Through books, through [[Video Essays]], and through series like [[For All Mankind]]. Channels like [[Kurzgesagt]], [[Exurb1a]], and [[Sisyphus 55]] live in an adjacent space and feed the same hunger.

## Sci-fi and his building instinct

Sci-fi trains him to take the [[Long-term Upside|long view]]. Builders who think in decades make different choices from builders who think in weeks. Reading sci-fi makes the long view feel native instead of forced. It reinforces his bias toward [[Ambition]] with real weight.
