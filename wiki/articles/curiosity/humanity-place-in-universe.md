# Humanity's Place in the Universe

This is one of the standing questions at the back of [[Hendrix]]'s mind. Not in a theatrical way. More like a quiet baseline that keeps his life calibrated. He returns to it through [[Space]], [[Astronomy]], [[Science Fiction]], and [[Philosophy]].

## Why the question matters to him

Hendrix is [[Introspection|introspective]] and serious about direction. Questions about scale and meaning keep his ambition honest. They stop him from confusing noise with importance and make it easier to spot when he is drifting into activity without purpose.

## How he thinks about it

- Humanity is small in absolute terms
- Small does not mean unimportant
- Perspective is a tool, not a mood
- Real weight comes from what you choose to build inside the brief window you get

## The existential angle

This is where the topic crosses into [[Existential Reflection]]. He does not want borrowed answers. He wants sharp personal ones. [[Nietzsche]] sits here too. So does good [[Science Fiction]].

## How it feeds his work

Holding the long view reinforces his bias toward [[Ambition]], [[Substance over Noise|substance over noise]], and [[Execution Over Talk|real execution]]. If time and scale are the true constraints, mediocre work is the most expensive choice.
