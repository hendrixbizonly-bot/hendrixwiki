# Eloquence

Eloquence is one of [[Hendrix]]'s stated language goals. He is already fluent in [[English]], but fluency is not what he is after. He wants to sound more natural, more articulate, more in command. Eloquence is the word he uses for the upgrade he wants.

## What it means to him

For Hendrix, eloquence is not decoration. It is not flowery speech or academic vocabulary. It is:

- Saying complex things clearly
- Phrasing thoughts sharply
- Sounding natural instead of translated
- Being in control of the language, not dragged by it

It lines up with his broader taste for [[Directness]], [[Clarity]], and [[No-BS]] communication. See [[Communication Preferences]].

## Not fluency, something above it

He already reads, writes, listens, and speaks fluently. Eloquence is the next layer. It is the difference between getting the message across and delivering it with weight.

## Why it matters

He is a builder who operates in English. He pitches, writes, brands, and leads in it. Every notch of eloquence gained is:

- Sharper [[Branding]] copy for [[Duodode]]
- Stronger client conversations
- Cleaner writing in long-form pieces
- More precise thinking in his own head

Better eloquence is better [[Leverage]].

## Related goals

Eloquence sits next to:

- [[Articulation]]: being able to say it clearly in the moment
- [[Precision in Speech]]: choosing the exact right word
- [[English Goals]]: the overall plan

Together these form the upgrade he is after.

## How he builds it

He does not chase eloquence through a single course. He chases it through [[Repetition]] and [[Exposure]]:

- [[Books]] and [[Long-form Thinking]]
- Sharp [[TV Series]] like [[Mad Men]], [[Succession]], [[Sherlock]]
- [[Video Essays]] from creators like [[Exurb1a]] and [[Sisyphus 55]]
- [[Essays]], [[Substack]], [[Medium]], [[The New York Times]]
- [[Writing]] regularly
- Wanting real correction on his mistakes rather than praise

## Connection to his system

Eloquence is a direct extension of how he thinks about [[Self-Development]] and [[Competence]]. You do not become eloquent by wanting it. You become eloquent by reading sharp writing, writing often, and caring when you fall short.
