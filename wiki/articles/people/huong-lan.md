# Huong Lan

## The Feeling Of That Time

Huong Lan belongs to a very specific emotional climate in my memory: high school afternoons, summer looseness, and the kind of friendship built through repeated ordinary presence. She was strong, decisive, and caring toward friends in a way that gave her a very clear outline.

Some people blur into the group once enough years pass. She does not. Her energy was too defined for that.

## After School, On The Way Home

One of the clearest recurring memories is going home after school with her and **[[Giang]]**. That rhythm matters to me more than a single big event would. It captures the daily side of **[[High School]]**, when friendship was still built from who you kept ending up beside.

There is a lot of truth in those repeated routes home. They tell you who belonged naturally in your day.

## The Drink Shop Summers

Her family ran a drink shop, and that place became part of the map of those years. **[[Giang]]**, **[[Duc Anh]]**, and I often went there. There were summers when we spent a lot of time in that orbit, and because of that, Huong Lan is tied not only to a person but to a setting.

That is one reason I connect her to **[[Friendship]]** and **[[Vietnam]]** so naturally. The memory is social, local, warm-weathered, and ordinary in the best way.

## Why She Belongs Here

She belongs here because she holds together several things I still value when I look back on those years: decisiveness, loyalty to friends, and a setting that became inseparable from the people in it.
