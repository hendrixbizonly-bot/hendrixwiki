# Mee

## Early Visual Memory

Mee belongs to the early visual memory of **[[Duodode]]** for me. She has been there since the earlier stretch of the team, and that matters because early collaborators leave fingerprints on the taste of a place before anyone is fully aware of it.

She is a brand designer, but I do not remember her through the title first. I remember her through color, proportion, sketching instinct, and a way of seeing that makes visual decisions feel less accidental.

## What Her Eye Does

Some designers can arrange a screen. Mee carries something deeper than that. She has a strong feel for color and a sketching ability that gives her ideas a lived texture before they become polished. That is why she belongs near **[[Branding]]**, **[[Taste]]**, and **[[Design Philosophy]]** in my mind.

Her visual sense helped shape many of the early works around us. Not in a flashy, self-promotional way. More in the quiet way that someone can shape the surface of a brand until the whole place starts looking more coherent because they were there.

## Working Together

One of the clearest shared references is the current **[[Duodode]]** website. Working on that with Mee and **[[Jack]]** made her role especially visible to me. The collaboration made it obvious how much of good visual work happens before the final screen: color decisions, rough directions, sketches, restraint, elimination.

Tools like **[[Figma]]** are only surfaces for that kind of judgment. Mee's value is the judgment itself.

## Why She Matters

She belongs here because she helped shape the visual layer of one of the most important working environments in my life. That alone gives her a permanent place in the network.

## Shared Moments

- Early Duodode work where her visual taste quietly set the standard for what looked right.
- Design conversations where color choices became the real center of the discussion.
- Sketch-driven collaboration that made ideas feel tangible before they were fully built.
- The current **[[Duodode]]** website work with **[[Jack]]**, where her eye was especially visible.
