# Tung

## How the Friendship Formed

Tung is one of the younger friends I connect to my university years. We were both part of **[[YEC]]**, but the friendship did not stay trapped inside club structure. It kept growing through ordinary time outside the official setting.

Now he is moving into a marketing career, which makes sense to me. He has always felt socially aware in a way that fits that direction.

## Cafe Sessions and Clothes

If I reduce the friendship to the atmosphere that surrounds it, I think of cafe sessions first. A lot of our connection was built there, talking about life in an unforced way and moving easily into conversations about **[[Fashion]]**.

That part matters because clothes were never just a shallow side topic between us. They were a way of talking about self-presentation, taste, confidence, and what kind of life each person was trying to grow into. That is why I connect him not only to fashion, but also to **[[Taste]]**.

## Why He Belongs Here

He belongs in the archive because he captures a softer social side of my university life. Not the loud public moments, but the part built from recurring conversation, shopping together, and seeing that someone has a visual instinct close enough to yours that it changes the flow of the friendship.

There is also a clear continuity between the club context, **[[Public Speaking]]**, and his current direction toward **[[Digital Marketing]]**. He sits in that transition naturally.

## Shared Moments

- Joining **[[YEC]]** in the same broader university period.
- Repeated cafe sessions where life and fashion became the main subjects.
- Shopping together and noticing how similar our visual instincts were.
- Conversations that moved easily between personal direction and future work.
