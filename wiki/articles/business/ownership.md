# Ownership

Ownership is one of [[Hendrix]]'s core preferences. He is biased toward owning the thing, not renting access to it. That preference runs through how he thinks about work, money, and long-term direction.

## What it means to him

Ownership is:

- Owning the business, not being an employee inside someone else's
- Owning the [[Systems]] he builds, not relying on platforms he does not control
- Owning decisions about his time, not outsourcing them to a schedule someone else sets
- Owning upside, not just salary

It is the opposite of dependency.

## Why it matters

Dependency has a ceiling. Ownership compounds. A salary is capped by headcount economics. An owned business, an owned audience, an owned asset, grows on its own curve. This lines up with his focus on [[Leverage]], [[Scale]], and [[Long-term Upside]].

## How it shows up now

- [[Duodode]] is owned
- The working stack and workflow are set by him
- [[Client Work]] is framed as partnership, not labor

The long arc pushes further in the same direction. See [[Agency-vs-Product]] and [[Millionaire Goal]].

## The trade-off

Ownership trades safety for upside. It demands more initiative, more risk, and more responsibility. Hendrix accepts that trade because the alternative, working without real ownership, conflicts with his [[Core Drives]].
