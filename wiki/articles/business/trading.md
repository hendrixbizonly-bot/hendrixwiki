# Trading

## Why It Belongs Here

Trading belongs in my archive less as a personal identity and more as a language that changed how I think. It forced me to take probability, timing, and downside more seriously. Once you absorb even a little of that mindset, it starts leaking into decisions far outside markets.

## What It Teaches

The useful part of trading is not fantasy about quick wins. It is the pressure to think in terms of exposure, patience, conviction, and error. It pushes against the childish desire to be certain before acting. Real conditions keep moving.

That is why trading sits close to **[[Ambition]]** and **[[Seriousness]]** for me. Without those, it collapses into impulse and fantasy very quickly.

## The Personal Association

My strongest association with this page is **[[Hoang Duc]]**. A lot of what first became legible to me around markets came through conversations with him. Those conversations also tied trading to **[[Crypto]]** and to the more grounded, outcomes-oriented side of **[[Entrepreneurship]]**.
