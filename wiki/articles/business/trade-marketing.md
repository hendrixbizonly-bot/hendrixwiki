# Trade Marketing

[[Trade Marketing]] was one of my first real encounters with work that was physical, practical, and impossible to fake. It brought me close to setup, logistics, promotions, and the kind of commercial effort that only counts if it gets executed properly.

That period matters because it stripped away abstraction. You cannot hide behind language when you are setting up booths, carrying materials, and dealing with the fatigue of long event days. It made me respect [[Real Output]] in a more grounded way.

It is also closely tied to [[Quang Anh]], because a lot of what I learned in that phase came less from discussion and more from watching how he worked. That experience later gave me a stronger contrast with [[Digital Marketing]], which pulled me toward a different surface of business.

Trade marketing taught pressure, stamina, and [[Responsibility for Outcomes]].
