# Mortality

## Essay

Mortality is one of the few ideas that can cut through performance immediately. It simplifies without making life smaller. A lot of noise loses its authority once I remember that time is finite, that bodies fail, and that the people I love are temporary too.

I do not experience that as a purely dark thought. It is part grief, part orientation. Mortality sharpens the question of what is actually worth doing, who matters, what kind of work deserves years, and what kind of life would feel honest if it ended earlier than expected. It makes fake urgency look cheap and real urgency look cleaner.

That is why books that touch death or survival often stay with me. They force a different scale of honesty. Underneath the productivity layer and the ambition layer, mortality keeps asking the oldest question: if time is limited, what deserves my attention while I still have it.
