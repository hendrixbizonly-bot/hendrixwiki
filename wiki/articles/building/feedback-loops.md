# Feedback Loops

Feedback loops are how [[Hendrix]] trusts that building is actually working. An idea in a head is theory. An idea shipped into reality generates feedback. Feedback produces improvement. That is the loop.

## The basic logic

- Build something
- Put it in reality
- Receive signal
- Adjust
- Repeat

Every cycle of that loop sharpens the product, the builder, and the [[Judgment]] behind both.

## Why they matter so much to him

Without feedback loops, building becomes guessing. Without guessing correction, guessing gets worse, not better. Feedback loops are the only way for [[Competence]] to compound.

He also trusts feedback more than theory because reality is honest. A user either engages or does not. A client either pays or does not. A design either works or does not. There is less room for [[Fluff]] here than in pre-shipping discussion.

## Tight loops vs loose loops

Tight loops (short time between build and feedback) are better. Long loops are dangerous because:

- Too many variables change before signal arrives
- Motivation decays
- Learning is diluted

He prefers anything that tightens the loop: smaller ships, faster releases, quicker client cycles.

## How he builds them

- [[Shipping]] often and small
- Watching how people actually use things
- Asking for direct responses from clients and collaborators
- Using [[Analytics]] and real data over impressions
- Reflecting ([[Introspection]]) on what shipped teaches him

## The trap

Running the loop without closing it. Shipping without looking at the result. Receiving feedback and not acting on it. That is motion without learning, which defeats the entire purpose.

## Connection to Duodode

Running [[Duodode]] is a continuous feedback loop. Every client project is a chance to sharpen process, positioning, and craft. The agency gets better only if the feedback is collected and used.

## The link to his philosophy

[[Ideas]] only matter when tested in reality. Feedback loops are the mechanism that tests them. This is where [[Philosophy]] and [[Build and Ship]] meet: in the actual loop between idea and reality.
