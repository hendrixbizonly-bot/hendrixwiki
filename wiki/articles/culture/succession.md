# Succession

Succession is likely the most aligned show in [[Hendrix]]'s rotation. Power, wealth, ambition, family, language, identity, every axis he thinks seriously about, inside one series. It is both a drama and a long lesson in how the very top of the business world actually talks and moves.

## What it gave him

- A brutal, honest look at [[Power]] and [[Money]]
- Some of the sharpest dialogue on modern television
- A feel for how real operators and heirs speak under pressure
- A serious take on [[Ambition]] and its costs
- Sharper [[English]] at a very high register

See [[American Pop Culture Influence]].

## Why it resonates

He is [[Ambition|ambitious]], [[ROI-Driven Thinking|ROI-driven]], and serious about [[Money]]. Succession is a show about people who are all three, taken to an extreme, and it does not flatter them. The show refuses the romance, which is exactly how he wants to look at wealth and power: seriously, not superficially.

## Themes he resonates with

- [[Power]] and succession in family businesses
- Wealth as a serious force, not decoration
- Ambition as both driver and trap
- Identity tied to legacy
- The cost of not building your own thing
- Language as status, weapon, and tell

## Language as weapon

The dialogue is one of the reasons the show matters to him. Every line is dense, specific, and loaded. It is a masterclass in [[Precision in Speech]], sharp insults, and exact phrasing. That feeds directly into his goals around [[Eloquence]] and [[Articulation]].

## What it clarifies

Succession is a long argument that inherited wealth without purpose is a trap. That sharpens his own direction: build something of his own, on his own terms, that does not rot the people inside it. It makes [[Duodode]] and the broader trajectory feel less like a career choice and more like a necessary one.

## Connection to his system

Succession connects to everything: [[Money]], [[Power]], [[Ambition]], [[Entrepreneurship]], [[Nietzsche]], and [[Self-Overcoming]]. It is a high-water mark for serious, grounded media about the exact world he is trying to operate in. It sits alongside [[Mad Men]], [[Game of Thrones]], and [[Ozark]] as the core of his serious TV diet.
